Decisions needed

These are blocking items — delay on any of them delays the build.

01
Main contractor vs. self-manage trades
A single MC takes programme responsibility and coordinates all trades, at a premium. Self-managing saves money but requires daily site presence and significant construction knowledge. Given the overlap of steelwork, roofing, and MEP, a MC is strongly recommended. If self-managing, you need a very experienced site manager.
End of May 2026
02
Appoint aluminium window supplier & place order
Dormer windows ×2, Juliette door ×1 double, gable end window ×1 — all custom aluminium. Lead times of 8–12 weeks are standard. If not ordered by mid-June, they won't arrive before the dormer needs closing in, leaving openings exposed through autumn.
Mid-June 2026
03
Living arrangements during roof demolition
The roof strip phase exposes the entire house. The first floor bedrooms directly below need sheeting and protection. Decide whether the household stays on the ground floor or relocates temporarily. The contractor can move faster if they don't need to protect occupied rooms below.

Risks & mitigations

Ranked by severity — monitor regularly and escalate early.

High — address immediately
Steel lead time slippage
Order structural steelwork the moment engineer's drawings are issued. Do not wait for MC appointment if drawings are ready. 4–6 week fabrication lead time is non-negotiable and sits on the critical path.
Roof not watertight before October
Treat 30 Sep as a hard deadline. Have temporary roofing membrane (Monarflex/tarpaulins) on standby from day one of roof demolition. Never leave the stripped roof unprotected overnight.
Unknown extent of water damage
The existing drawings and scope both reference water damage from roof leaks. Until strip-out is complete you won't know the full extent. Build 10–15% cost contingency and 2 weeks programme buffer specifically for remedial works.
Window lead time — dormer left open
Aluminium windows on 8–12 week lead time. If the dormer structure is complete but windows haven't arrived, you need temporary hoarding or a membrane solution. Do not leave openings unprotected. Appoint supplier now.
Medium — monitor actively
Trade availability in summer peak
Good structural carpenters, roofers, and plumbers book out 6–8 weeks ahead in summer. Appoint all key trades now. Availability is as important as price — ask each contractor for their earliest start date.
Scaffolding coordination with neighbours
Flank elevation shows semi-detached neighbour (EGA003). Notify 217a and 217b in writing before scaffold is erected. Party Wall Agreement confirmed not required.
Mains moling disruption
Moling from drive to house will disrupt supply. Coordinate timing when you have alternative water access. Thames Water may require advance notice — check before booking contractor.
Rosemary clay tile supply
Clay tiles can have batch lead times and colour variation between batches. Order all tiles at once. Confirm availability before demolition starts — you can't strip the old roof without new tiles on order.
Lower — monitor as programme develops
Skip road permit delays
Apply for Council road permit as soon as MC is appointed. Ealing can take 2–3 weeks. Don't let waste management hold up site clearance.
Decorative cornicing damage
Photograph and document all existing cornicing before works begin. Brief every trade. Protect sections adjacent to work areas. Specialist cornice restorers are available but expensive — prevention is far cheaper.
Building Control sign-off
Building Regs apply to structural works, fire doors, electrical upgrade, and drainage changes. Notify Building Control before works start and agree inspection stages. Get sign-off stage by stage — don't assume at completion.

All trades required. Get 2–3 quotes for each. Ask for earliest start date alongside price.
